Film Context: It was written specifically to match the plot of City of Angels, where Nic Cage’s character, an angel, falls in love with a human and chooses mortality over immortality. The background of the video is my acrylic painting depicting the narrative of a fallen angel. 
The flowers are designed to symbolize iris flowers, which naturally appear in shades of blue, yellow, and white. These colors are intentionally echoed in the orbs to create visual cohesion throughout the piece. A muted color palette was chosen to convey a sense of softness, melancholy, and introspection, reinforcing the emotional weight of the narrative.
The orbs and flowers were drawn in Procreate and they pulse and drift to the music, creating a crossmodal experience. The piece is structured into multiple stages, switching between from orbs to flowers during the chorus, switching back to orbs after the chorus, further development with a second chorus, then a climax for the final chorus, and finally the flowers slowly fall down. These stages convey the narrative of the meaning behind the song. 

All visual elements shown in this video were created by me.
